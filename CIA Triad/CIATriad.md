## The Importance of Protecting Digital Data

In the past, most information was stored on physical paper. Today, that same information exists as digital data stored on systems and transmitted across networks.

Without proper security controls, digital data can face serious risks. It may be exposed to unauthorized individuals, altered without permission, or become unavailable when it is needed most. As a result, protecting digital data has become a fundamental requirement for governments, organizations, and individuals.

Example: Data transmitted from one location to another may be intercepted and modified during transmission.

## What Does Security Really Mean?

Security is not simply about preventing attacks or deploying security tools. In cybersecurity, being secure means ensuring that specific conditions are maintained for digital data.

These conditions are defined by three core principles:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

Together, these principles form the foundation of cybersecurity. They are known as the **CIA Triad**.

Nearly every concept in cybersecurity relates to protecting or attacking one of these three pillars. Whether defending systems or analyzing threats, the objective ultimately revolves around maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital data.

## Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that sensitive data is accessible only to authorized individuals. If confidentiality is compromised, unauthorized parties may gain access to the data, potentially resulting in financial loss, privacy violations, or legal consequences.

Imagine having a private discussion with a friend about a personal matter, and an unknown person deliberately listens and later uses that information to manipulate you. In this case, information meant to remain private was accessed by someone without authorization. This directly compromises confidentiality. To protect confidentiality in the future, you might choose to have such conversations in a secure area and remain aware of your surroundings.

In the digital world, consider logging into your social media account while connected to a public Wi-Fi network in a coffee shop. If someone intercepts your credentials during the login process and gains access to your account, confidentiality has been compromised. To protect confidentiality in digital systems, mechanisms such as encryption and access controls are used.

### Examples

| Situation                                                                 | Confidentiality Achieved? |
|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Your Gmail credentials are written on sticky notes on your office table | No                        |
| Internal company documents are accessible only to employees who need them | Yes                       |
| One of your personal documents is publicly available on the internet     | No                        |



## Integrity

Integrity ensures that data cannot be modified by unauthorized individuals. Without integrity, data cannot be trusted. Unauthorized changes may lead to serious or even dangerous consequences.

Imagine a teacher assigns you a good grade on an exam, but someone modifies the grade before it is submitted to the examination authority. This compromises the integrity of your academic record. To prevent this, the teacher might implement verification procedures before final submission.

In the digital world, suppose you initiate a bank transfer from your mobile device. If an attacker intercepts the transaction and modifies the recipient account details, the integrity of the transaction has been breached. Various cryptographic and validation mechanisms are used in digital systems to maintain integrity.

### Examples

| Situation                                                       | Integrity Achieved? |
|-----------------------------------------------------------------|---------------------|
| Data changed through authorized approval                        | Yes                 |
| Attendance records modified after being locked by the teacher  | No                  |
| Order price modified before checkout                            | No                  |

## Availability

Availability ensures that data and services are accessible to authorized users when needed. Although it is the third pillar of the CIA Triad, it is equally important as confidentiality and integrity.

Many organizations rely heavily on digital services. If those services become unavailable, business operations may halt, resulting in significant financial and operational losses.

Imagine depositing money in a bank that stores it securely, but the bank is closed the day you need access due to a power outage. Even though the money is safe, it is inaccessible. To ensure availability, the bank might deploy backup power systems to maintain operations during outages.

In the digital world, availability can be compromised by attacks such as Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS), where attackers overwhelm a system with excessive requests, causing it to crash or become unresponsive. Although no data may be stolen or altered, the disruption itself can cause severe damage. To maintain availability, organizations implement traffic management systems, redundancy, and failover mechanisms.

### Examples

| Situation                                                        | Availability Achieved? |
|------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------|
| Critical services disrupted due to software installation         | No                     |
| Company website goes offline during business hours               | No                     |
| All systems are accessible to employees during working hours     | Yes                    |
